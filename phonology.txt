#autumnal
> phonology

the phonology of áthid will take on the form of a largely fricative and alveolar-based inventory for consonants and a lot of work on /a/ and /é/ for vowels:

>>consonants
plosives — [p] [b] [t] [d] [k~g] [ʔ]
nasals - [m] [ɲ] [n]
fricative - [f] [θ] [s~z] [ʃ] [ʒ] [ç] [h]
lat. frica. — [ɬ]
approximant — [w] [ʋ] [ɹ~ɾ]* [j]
lat. approx. — [l]

[allophones]: z~ʒ, ç~h
*these depend heavily on accent and season. /ɹ/ is often realized as /ʋ/ or /w/.

>notes

vowels:
- "long" vowels <e, u, á, y and ó> are realized [x:].
- all vowels are lengthened when at the end of a word.
- these long vowels are shortened when the following consonant is a nasal or a geminated form: ámm (earth) is pronounced /amm/, not /ɑ:m/.

consonants:
- /h/ after a nasal consonant geminates the consonant.
- /h/ after a fricative can take the form of a glottal stop [ʔ]
